Indian & Metric Units Explained

Tola is used in India for gold and silver. 1 tola = 11.664 grams. Bigha is a land area unit; it varies by state (e.g. UP ~27,000 sq ft, Bihar ~27,220 sq ft). We use 27,000 sq ft as a common reference—always confirm with local standards for land deals. Guntha is used in Maharashtra, Karnataka, etc.: 1 guntha = 1,089 sq ft. Acre: 1 acre = 43,560 sq ft. For length, 1 km ≈ 0.621371 miles.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How many grams is 1 tola?

    1 tola = 11.664 grams (standard in India for gold/silver).

  • How much is 1 bigha in square feet?

    It varies by state. In many regions 1 bigha ≈ 27,000 sq ft (e.g. UP). We use a common reference; check local standards for land.

  • What is 1 guntha in square feet?

    1 guntha = 1,089 square feet (used in Maharashtra, Karnataka, etc. for land).
