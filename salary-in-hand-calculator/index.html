How This Salary In-Hand Calculator Works

This tool estimates your salary in hand India from your annual Cost to Company (CTC)—from an employee point of view: what you take home each month. CTC includes your gross salary plus employer contributions (employer PF, gratuity). Your in-hand is gross pay minus: employee Provident Fund (12% of basic), professional tax (we use ₹200/month; state rules vary), and income tax. You can choose old regime or new regime; tax slabs used here are for FY 2025–26 (assessment year). Slabs may change; we keep logic in one place so it can be updated when notified.

Assumptions: Basic = 40% of monthly gross (CTC/12). We do not apply HRA exemption or other deductions in the tax calculation, so the result is a conservative estimate. For exact figures, use your salary slip or a tax consultant.

Common salary scenarios: Use this to compare job offers (e.g. 6 L vs 10 L CTC), plan EMIs and savings, or see how much of your CTC actually lands in your account. Focus is on take-home pay for Indian private-sector employees.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How is salary in hand calculated from CTC in India?

    Salary in hand = Monthly gross (CTC/12) minus employee PF (12% of basic), professional tax, and income tax as per Indian tax slabs.

  • What is deducted from CTC to get in-hand salary?

    Employee PF (12% of basic), professional tax (state-wise, often ₹200/month), and income tax (as per old regime slabs) are deducted from your gross salary to get in-hand.

  • Is gratuity deducted from salary?

    No. Gratuity is paid by the employer and is not deducted from your monthly salary. It is part of CTC but does not reduce your in-hand pay.
