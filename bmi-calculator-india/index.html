How This BMI Calculator Works

This BMI calculator India computes your Body Mass Index using the standard formula: BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)². BMI is a screening tool that estimates body fat based on height and weight. It's widely used in India for health assessments, insurance, and fitness tracking.

BMI Categories for Indian Adults (as per ICMR guidelines): Underweight: BMI below 18.5 (may indicate malnutrition or health issues). Normal weight: BMI 18.5–22.9 (healthy range for most Indians). Overweight: BMI 23–24.9 (increased health risk). Obese: BMI 25 and above (higher risk of diabetes, heart disease, hypertension). Indian population has higher health risks at lower BMI compared to Caucasians, so the thresholds are adjusted.

Common Indian use cases: Health checkups—doctors use BMI to assess weight-related health risks. Insurance—some policies consider BMI for premium calculation. Fitness goals—track progress when losing or gaining weight. Government exams—some physical tests have BMI requirements. Pregnancy—BMI helps determine healthy weight gain during pregnancy. Note: BMI doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution. Athletes, bodybuilders, and elderly may have misleading BMI. Always consult a healthcare professional for a complete health assessment.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How is BMI calculated?

    BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)². For example, if you weigh 70 kg and are 1.75 m tall, BMI = 70 / (1.75 × 1.75) = 22.86, which is in the normal range for Indians.

  • What is a normal BMI for Indians?

    For Indian adults, normal BMI is 18.5–22.9. Underweight: below 18.5, Overweight: 23–24.9, Obese: 25 and above. Indian population has higher health risks at lower BMI than Caucasians, so thresholds are adjusted.

  • Is BMI accurate for everyone?

    BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic. It doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution. Athletes, bodybuilders, and elderly may have misleading BMI. Consult a doctor for a complete health assessment.

  • Can I use BMI for children?

    BMI calculation for children and teens uses age and sex-specific percentiles, not fixed categories. This calculator is for adults (18+). For children, consult a pediatrician or use age-specific BMI charts.

  • What if my BMI is in the overweight or obese range?

    If your BMI indicates overweight or obesity, consider consulting a doctor or nutritionist. They can help create a safe weight management plan. Focus on balanced diet, regular exercise, and lifestyle changes rather than crash diets.
