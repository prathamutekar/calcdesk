Logic & Data Sources (Updated February 2026)

This government exam eligibility checker uses static rules based on official notifications and typical eligibility criteria for major Indian government exams. Data is compiled from SSC (ssc.gov.in), UPSC (upsc.gov.in), RRB (indianrailways.gov.in), IBPS (ibps.in), and other official sources as of February 2026.

UPSC Exams

  • Civil Services (IAS/IPS/IFS): Age 21–32 (General), 21–35 (OBC), 21–37 (SC/ST), 21–32 (EWS). Graduation required. Attempts: 6 (General), 9 (OBC), unlimited (SC/ST).
  • CDS (Combined Defence Services): Age 20–24 (Air Force), 19–25 (IMA/INA), 19–25 (OTA). Graduation required (except for IMA/INA which require 12th).
  • NDA (National Defence Academy): Age 16.5–19.5 years. 12th pass (for Army/Navy/Air Force).
  • CAPF (Central Armed Police Forces): Age 20–25 (General), 20–28 (OBC), 20–30 (SC/ST). Graduation required.

SSC Exams

  • SSC CGL (Combined Graduate Level): Age 18–32 (General), 18–35 (OBC/EWS), 18–37 (SC/ST). Graduation required. Age varies by post.
  • SSC CHSL (Combined Higher Secondary Level): Age 18–27 (General), 18–30 (OBC), 18–32 (SC/ST), 18–27 (EWS). 12th pass required.
  • SSC MTS (Multi-Tasking Staff): Age 18–27 (General), 18–30 (OBC), 18–32 (SC/ST), 18–27 (EWS). 10th pass required.
  • SSC JE (Junior Engineer): Age 18–32 (General), 18–35 (OBC/EWS), 18–37 (SC/ST). Diploma/Degree in Engineering required.
  • SSC GD Constable: Age 18–23 (General), 18–26 (OBC), 18–28 (SC/ST), 18–23 (EWS). 10th pass required.

Railway Exams (RRB)

  • RRB NTPC (Graduate posts): Age 18–33 (General), 18–36 (OBC), 18–38 (SC/ST), 18–33 (EWS). Graduation required.
  • RRB NTPC (12th posts): Age 18–30 (General), 18–33 (OBC), 18–35 (SC/ST), 18–30 (EWS). 12th pass required.
  • RRB Group D: Age 18–33 (General), 18–36 (OBC), 18–38 (SC/ST), 18–33 (EWS). 10th pass required.
  • RRB ALP (Assistant Loco Pilot): Age 18–30 (General), 18–33 (OBC), 18–35 (SC/ST), 18–30 (EWS). 10th + ITI or Diploma required.
  • RRB JE (Junior Engineer): Age 18–33 (General), 18–36 (OBC), 18–38 (SC/ST), 18–33 (EWS). Diploma/Degree in Engineering required.

Banking Exams (IBPS)

  • IBPS PO (Probationary Officer): Age 20–30 (General), 20–33 (OBC), 20–35 (SC/ST), 20–30 (EWS). Graduation required.
  • IBPS Clerk: Age 20–28 (General), 20–31 (OBC), 20–33 (SC/ST), 20–28 (EWS). Graduation required.
  • IBPS SO (Specialist Officer): Age 20–30 (General), 20–33 (OBC), 20–35 (SC/ST), 20–30 (EWS). Graduation + relevant specialization required.
  • SBI PO: Age 21–30 (General), 21–33 (OBC), 21–35 (SC/ST), 21–30 (EWS). Graduation required.
  • SBI Clerk: Age 20–28 (General), 20–31 (OBC), 20–33 (SC/ST), 20–28 (EWS). Graduation required.

Other Major Exams

  • LIC AAO (Assistant Administrative Officer): Age 21–30 (General), 21–33 (OBC), 21–35 (SC/ST), 21–30 (EWS). Graduation required.
  • LIC ADO (Apprentice Development Officer): Age 21–30 (General), 21–33 (OBC), 21–35 (SC/ST), 21–30 (EWS). Graduation required.
  • RBI Grade B: Age 21–30 (General), 21–33 (OBC), 21–35 (SC/ST), 21–30 (EWS). Graduation required.
  • RBI Assistant: Age 20–28 (General), 20–31 (OBC), 20–33 (SC/ST), 20–28 (EWS). Graduation required.
  • State PSC: Age limits vary by state (typically 21–35 for General, with relaxations). Graduation usually required. Check state-specific notifications.

Note: Age relaxations apply for reserved categories, ex-servicemen, and PWD candidates. Some exams have post-specific age limits. Always refer to the official notification for the exact exam cycle you are applying for.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the age limit for SSC CGL?

    SSC CGL general category age limit is 18–32 years. OBC/EWS up to 35, SC/ST up to 37. Age limits vary by specific post within CGL.

  • What is the age limit for UPSC Civil Services?

    UPSC Civil Services (IAS/IPS/IFS) age limit: General 21–32, OBC 21–35, SC/ST 21–37, EWS 21–32. Number of attempts also vary by category (6 for General, 9 for OBC, unlimited for SC/ST).

  • What is the age limit for RRB NTPC?

    RRB NTPC has different age limits: Graduate posts 18–33 (General), 18–36 (OBC), 18–38 (SC/ST). 12th pass posts: 18–30 (General), 18–33 (OBC), 18–35 (SC/ST).

  • What is the age limit for IBPS PO?

    IBPS PO age limit: 20–30 (General), 20–33 (OBC), 20–35 (SC/ST), 20–30 (EWS). Graduation is required.

  • Where are exam eligibility rules taken from?

    Rules are based on official notifications from SSC (ssc.gov.in), UPSC (upsc.gov.in), RRB (indianrailways.gov.in), IBPS (ibps.in), and state boards (updated as of February 2026). Always confirm with the latest official notification for the specific exam cycle.

  • Do age limits change every year?

    Age limits are generally stable but can change. Some exams adjust age limits based on reference dates (e.g., "as of August 1, 2026"). Always check the official notification for the exact exam cycle.
