Understanding CTC Components (HR / Offer-Letter View)

This CTC breakup India tool explains how CTC is structured for offer letters and HR. Basic salary is the core pay used for PF and gratuity; in India it is often 40–50% of gross. HRA (House Rent Allowance) is tax-exempt up to limits if the employee pays rent; we use 50% of basic for metro and 40% for non-metro. Special allowance is the balance of gross after basic and HRA; it is fully taxable.

Employer PF is 12% of basic (employer contribution to EPF). Gratuity is 4.81% of basic per year, paid by the employer; it is part of CTC but not in the monthly slip. This calculator gives a standard salary structure India; companies may use different ratios. For take-home pay, use our Salary In-Hand Calculator.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is included in CTC in India?

    CTC includes basic salary, HRA, special allowance, employer PF, gratuity, and other allowances. It is the total cost the company spends on you per year.

  • How is basic salary calculated from CTC?

    Basic is often 40–50% of gross salary. This calculator uses 40% of monthly gross (CTC/12) as basic for PF and gratuity calculations.

  • What is the difference between CTC and in-hand salary?

    CTC is total cost to company. In-hand is what you receive after deducting employee PF, professional tax, and income tax from your gross salary.
